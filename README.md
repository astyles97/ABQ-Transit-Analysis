# ABQ Transit Analysis

A data analysis project seeking to answer the question if making ART free increased employment in Albuquerque, New Mexico from 2019 to 2026.

## The Question

Did making Albuquerque's public transit system, ABQ RIDE, free for all increase employment in the city? And can we see that increase more prominantly in certain sectors?

### Data Sources

Data is pulled from transit.dot.gov City of Albuquerque Transit Agency Profile and New Mexico Department of Workforce Solutions County Employment & Wages.

## Results

###Data Visialization:
https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/adam.styles1536/viz/2019-2024AlbuquerqueRidershipandEmploymentIndex/Sheet1#1

In October of 2022, Albuquerque made its public bus transportation system fare free. Since that initiative began, ridership increased by 20%. Of the 23,800 ABQ RIDE daily riders, 88% live in a household making less than $35,000 annually. The data presented in this chart shows the correlation of the employment rate to bus ridership. Industries related to food service, retail,  health care, arts/entertainment, transportation, finance, and public administration. These were chosen on the criteria that they are lower-paying jobs or jobs located in the city downtown.


The impacts of the Covid 19 Pandemic play a clear role in the dramatic plunge in both data sets. Employment recovered faster than ridership, likely related to limited service during this time period.


Both ridership and employment are indexed to 2019Q1 (= 100), representing pre-pandemic baseline levels. Values above 100 indicate growth beyond baseline; values below 100 indicate decline. This allows two metrics measured in different units, bus trips and jobs, to be compared on the same scale.
